  • Master Data
    Master Data

    This relates to data that is used as the basis for multiple other elements of data production (e.g. the dates of the academic year, the heads of faculty, the line management structure) that is not simply maintained as a matter of course / part of MIS operations. Cf. MDM (Master Data Management).

    See the trends in data governance meetings, but this should cover (1) the academic year dates (including INSET - i.e. it is possible sessions. Ideally by year group), (2) Heads of Faculty/Subject (for comparison to course map, exam reports etc., (3) classes of staff (ability to use 'for each of teacher's classes' construct, such as with a loosely coupled pair of apps. One calls the second with parameters (in a loop), passing the second the instructions. Means that the second can be used by itself, too. Consider this approach for 6th form timetables...)., (4)
      Monitoring:
    • Use of academic year to analyse behaviour by week/month/term, controlled for possible sessions. Note that some months will be controlled by a great degree (December = 0.5?) so should be aware of greater fluctuation potentia. Compare this to uncontrolled (my pretty != useful chart...)
